My Foundations Learning Plan

My career path way

I want to be a full time developer. I would like to first land a good junior position in a decent sized company so I can perfect and solidify what I have learned at EDA and expand that learning to new horizons. During that time I would like to get some clarity on what area of coding I am best at or enjoy more. Then I would like to land a job in a higher position where I can get experience in a more specialized area of coding and become solid in that area. And from there I don't know. What I do know is that I want to keep up with the constant evolving tools of a developer.

My strengths concerning learning

I am very curious, patient and persistent. When I am working on something and I want to accomplish A, I will only settle for B temporarily. I will go to bed and my mind will keep thinking about ways to accomplish A. The next morning I will try every possible way that my mind came up with. I Google a lot and I like to compare different functions to get an understanding of which one will best suit what I am trying to accomplish. In this process I learn a lot of different things and sometimes I learn that actually A is not the best outcome so I end up pivoting to C, D or Z..

My limitations concerning learning

My limitations are that I do not enjoy asking for help. It is something that I haven't had to do much in my life. Usually I figure out things by myself. So asking for help doesn't come naturally to me. That is why I settle temporarily for option B while I find the best way to accomplish A. I only ask for help when I am running out of time.

A human skill that I lack and long to have

I would like to be more social. I am capable of interacting well with anyone in a 1-1 setting or even in a small group of 3 - 4 people. But I struggle in large groups. It makes me anxious.

Maybe it's because I do not like being in the spotlight. Or because I am more of an observer - I love listening to people and understanding their views on a matter. Or maybe it is because English is my second language and in spontaneous settings where the conversation moves fast I can get a bit lost.

SSmall talk is not my thing. I am terrible at it. So networking at social events is my worst nightmare. It isn't a fear of public speaking, I have no problems doing that. I can address myself to a big audience when I know what I want to communicate and how to communicate it - like at a court hearing, or if I have to give a speech somewhere. .

My commitment to how I will manage my workload at EDA

I will go through the material required for each lesson/challenge in time so that when I have to work with other people I have an understanding of what we are doing, what the goal is and some ideas of how to work towards that goal. And also an understanding of the things I don't understand so I can verbalize them and ask for support in those areas.

If things build up and start to get to me, I will be honest about it to my team members and facilitator so we can discuss what is the most urgent thing I need to focus on and get done, and what are things that I could catch up on in my spare time.

My commitment to seeking help on time

First I will Google the issue. If I don't find the answer in 20 minutes, I will ask my peers. And if they can't help me, then I will ask a facilitator.

What I expect from the facilitation team

I expect them to critique my code so I can improve. I also expect them to point me in a direction when I am lost or when there is a better solution I could use.

My scheduling information during Foundations

I will be busy with the kids every morning between 8.00am - 9.10am and in the afternoon/evening from 2.20pm till 8.00pm (I pick them-up, do homework, make dinner, play with them and get them ready for bed). Also Sunday mornings and some Saturday afternoons is our family time. .

My scheduling information during the Bootcamp

My husband will be fully involved with the kids. But I want to be present for them once I get home till their bed time which is at 8.00pm. I will attempt to respect our family time but I understand that it might not always be possible.

My Bootcamp Learning Plan

What is your long term goal and/or career pathway?

I would love to join a software development firm, or a big tech company so I can improve the abilities learnt during bootcamp. After a couple of years I would love to take my knowledge to smaller companies that are looking to develop/improve new products.

What do you think your biggest strengths and limitations will be in Bootcamp?

My strengths are that I am very curious, patient and persistent. I love to learn new things and to update my knowledge and habits. My limitations are my current lack of knowledge, and my resistance to ask for help. Although I think both things have and will keep improving. Regarding asking for help, I find it less challenging when the teacher is right in front of me and the class is a small intimate group, so I think during bootcamp this limitation should improve.

What do you think your biggest non-technical challenge at Bootcamp will be?

I think the social aspect of it will be hard for me. I love my solitude, so having to be social for 8+ hours from Monday - Friday for nine weeks sounds intense.

What non-technical skills - human skills - would you like to see developed in yourself while at Dev Academy?

I think I would like to be able to communicate my needs/challenges. I'm not used to asking for things even if I really do need something (like an extension, a different example, a second explanation, etc.). When something makes me uncomfortable I tend to just deal with it in the hopes that it will soon pass, instead of speaking up for myself. I think it is important that I learn to communicate those needs/ challenges to my team/tutors/future employers so I can be in a better position to keep learning.

What are your expectations from the Bootcamp team?

I expect them to explain something to me in as many different ways as it might be necessary until I get the concept being taught. I would also like to receive individualised suggestions of things I need to practice on.

What are your expectations of yourself on Bootcamp

I expect myself to be responsible when learning. For me, this translates to making sure I understand at least the essentials of each topic taught in class. It is unrealistic to expect that I will master each topic the first time I am exposed to it. But if I can get at least the essentials, I can deep dive into each topic in my own time. So this means that I must ensure I do the extra work in my spare time so my new knowledge doesn't stay superficial. In order to achieve this goal my commitment to myself is to:

    Take notes during the lessons Ask questions in class to my teachers and classmates Make sure that my team members also understand the topics and projects we are working on together Over the weekend study the topics learnt during the week Practice coding over the weekend Seek feedback from my teachers/pairs
